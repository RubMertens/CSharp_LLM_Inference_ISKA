## Colour Usage per Audience

| Audience | Primary | Secondary allowed |
|---|---|---|
| **Handelsdrukwerk en signing** | Wit, Info Support Blauw, Navy | *None* -- primary colours only. Background is white. |
| **Business** | Wit, Info Support Blauw, Navy | Groen, Geel, Roze (max 2 per item). Photo backgrounds may use primary + max 2 secondary. |
| **Intern** | Wit, Info Support Blauw, Navy | Felblauw, Rood (max 2 per item). For text in Arial, black may also be used. |
| **Arbeidsmarkt** | Wit, Info Support Blauw, Navy, *plus* Felblauw as 4th primary | Groen, Geel, Roze, Paars, Rood (max 2 per item). Most colour variation. |

## Gradients

The guide mentions that secondary colours "versterken elkaar en werken goed in gradients" (reinforce each other and work well in gradients). Specific gradient definitions (start/end stops) are **not provided** -- this is left to the designer's discretion within the colour palette constraints.

## Constraints

- "Info Support blauw" is used everywhere as the signature colour.
- Colour build depends on the medium: digital uses HEX/RGB; print uses CMYK/PMS and differs between coated and uncoated paper (see Chapter 10).
- Background is always white for Handelsdrukwerk en signing.
- Photo backgrounds: photo + Info Support Blauw + Navy + white, optionally plus max 2 secondary colours.
